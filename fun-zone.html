Coined Concepts

Original terminology invented by R U Narasimham to deepen understanding of English

🔀

Versonym

A word that serves as more than one part of speech. Example: "Water" can be a noun, adjective, or verb.

Learn More →
🎯

Exclunym

A word that belongs to only one part of speech — the opposite of a Versonym. Example: "Javelin" is always a noun.

Learn More →
📝

Factonym

A creative acronym-style expression where each letter of a name expands into a factual, descriptive phrase about that person or thing.

Learn More →
🧠

Mnemogram

A memory-aid sentence or story that uses punctuation marks creatively to illustrate their usage in memorable ways.
