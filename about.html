The book, the author, and the mission behind English Grammar Access Made Easy

📖 The Book

E-GAME — English Grammar Access Made Easy is a comprehensive English grammar guide built on the innovative SVAPTIP framework. It covers basic and advanced grammar topics with logical illustrations, a question bank with answer keys, and the creative PALPS word game.

✍️ The Author

Sri R U Narasimham — Word Analyst — has a deep passion for teaching English. He runs the WhatsApp group "Learn English With Fun" (LEWF), founded on 01 June 2020, now in its 5th year, comprising members from varied backgrounds including English professors, students, homemakers, and English enthusiasts.

He serves as an honorary professor of English at Satya Institute of Technology and Management (SITAM), Vizianagaram, where he helps enhance students' communication skills through creative online and offline sessions.

He invented several innovative concepts:

  • PALPS (Pick And Learn Parts of Speech) — a word game to learn parts of speech
  • SPELL SPREAD — making logical phrases and sentences with given words
  • SLING (Sentence LINking Game) — a unique sentence-building game
  • EITHER (End the sentence wITH the same lettER) — sentence building with variation
  • Coined the terms: Exclunym, Versonym, Factonym & Mnemogram
  • Invented the concept of Responsive Sentence

He also published "Probe the Globe" (acronyms for all 194 countries) and "Riddles by RUNS".

📚 The SVAPTIP Framework

Seven essential steps to start the English grammar journey:

  • S — Sentences: kinds, parts, patterns, subject-verb agreement
  • V — Verbs: classification, modals, non-finites, phrasal verbs
  • A — Articles: A, An, The — usage rules & omissions
  • P — Parts of Speech: 8 parts — noun to interjection
  • T — Tenses: 12 tenses with purpose, construction & examples
  • I — Interrogatives & Imperatives: WH-words, question tags
  • P — Punctuation: commas, colons, apostrophes & capitalization

💡 Key Concepts

Versonym: A word that serves as more than one part of speech. For example, "WATER" can be a noun ("Water is colourless") and a verb ("Water the plants"). The book covers 100 such words in the PALPS game.

Exclunym: A word that belongs to only one part of speech — the opposite of a Versonym.

Responsive Sentence: A new concept invented by the author — a creative approach to sentence construction.
